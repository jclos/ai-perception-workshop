SICSA Workshop on Perception in Artificial Intelligence

April 11th, 2018 in Aberdeen, Scotland

AI systems in general need to perceive something from their inputs (whether sensory in the usual sense, or in terms of large volumes of (perhaps transient) digital data, perhaps from sensors, or from the WWW) so that they can apply their intelligence to producing useful outputs. We are looking to bring together researchers working on AI related vision, sound/speech (and, if there are any, touch, olfaction, proprioception), as well as those using perception in a broader sense, for example using AI to find useful information from large data volumes (perceiving applied to Big Data, including textual data). What are the problems in reducing huge volumes of inputs (whether pixels or words or digital samples) to make them usable by AI systems? What sorts of AI systems can process these inputs, and what sorts of processing is appropriate?

This workshop will take place in the Robert Gordon University, in the Sir Ian Wood Building, on April 11th 2018.

Call for papers

We welcome submissions on the following topics:

  • Physical perception in AI (computer vision, sound/speech processing, other sensors)
  • Abstract perception in AI (text sensing, social sensing)

Submission

Please send short exploratory papers of no more than 4 pages plus 1 for references at fakeemailaddress@rgu.ac.uk. The deadline for submission is April 4th at Midnight.

Getting to RGU

The Sir Ian Wood Building is located in the Garthdee campus of the Robert Gordon University. It is easily accessible by bus (bus 1, 1A and 2 in the city centre) or by taxi (15 minutes from the train station).
